# Landing Copy

## Messaging Strategy

The landing page optimizes for three emotions in sequence:
1. **Recognition** — "Oh, this is about a problem I have"
2. **Trust** — "This seems credible, not gimmicky"
3. **Urgency** — "I should try this now, it's free and fast"

## Headline Iterations

| Version | Copy | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| v1 | "Find wasted AI spend before your next invoice." | **Selected.** Specific, urgent, outcome-oriented |
| v2 | "Your AI stack is leaking money." | Too aggressive, might alienate |
| v3 | "Audit your AI spend in 60 seconds." | Feature-focused, not outcome-focused |
| v4 | "How much are you overspending on AI tools?" | Question format, good for ads, not for hero |

## Subheadline

"Enter your AI tools and subscriptions. Get a deterministic audit with real savings opportunities — no guesswork, no AI hallucinations, no sales calls."

### Why this works:
- "deterministic" → signals technical credibility
- "no AI hallucinations" → directly addresses skepticism about AI tools
- "no sales calls" → removes friction fear

## Trust Signals

Three cards below the fold:
1. **"No signup required"** — Removes the biggest friction point
2. **"Results in 60 seconds"** — Sets clear time expectation
3. **"100% deterministic"** — Differentiates from AI-generated advice

## CTA Copy

- Primary: "Audit Your Stack →"
- Secondary: "Start Free Audit"
- Supporting: "Results in 60 seconds · 100% free"

## Voice & Tone

- Professional but not corporate
- Confident but not salesy
- Technical but not academic
- Direct. No fluff. Every word earns its place.
