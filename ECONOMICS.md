# Economics

## Unit Economics Model

### User Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Phase 1 (Product-led): ~$0 (organic distribution via shareability)
- Phase 2 (Content-led): ~$5-15 per audit (content production cost / audits generated)
- Phase 3 (Paid): TBD

### Lead Value
- Each audit that converts to email capture is a qualified lead for Credex
- Credex sells credit procurement services — typical contract value $5K-50K
- If StackTrim delivers even 10 qualified leads/month, the ROI is significant

### Infrastructure Cost
- Vercel hobby/pro tier: $0-20/mo
- Supabase free tier: $0/mo (up to 50K monthly active users)
- Domain: ~$12/year
- **Total Day 1 cost: <$20/mo**

## Revenue Model (If StackTrim were a standalone product)

### Freemium Tiers

| Tier | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Single audit, basic recommendations |
| Pro | $29/mo | Unlimited audits, PDF export, historical tracking |
| Team | $99/mo | Team workspace, shared audits, benchmark data |

### Revenue Drivers
1. **PDF exports** — Premium feature, high perceived value for sharing with leadership
2. **Historical tracking** — Re-audit monthly to track optimization progress
3. **Benchmark data** — "How does your AI spend compare to similar companies?"

## Key Assumption

The product is currently positioned as a **lead-generation tool for Credex**, not an independent SaaS. The economics above show both paths are viable.

## Break-Even Analysis

At the current infrastructure cost (~$20/mo), StackTrim breaks even with essentially any level of usage. The real cost is engineering time, which is justified by the lead generation value.
